The Interlock Protocol

In solution to potential Eves intercepting the flow of information between the Bobs and Alices of the world, we luckily may lean on the interlock protocol [1]. The diagram can be read in the following order:

  1. A sends Public Key A to B
  2. E intercepts Public Key A
  3. E sends Public Key E to B
  4. B sends Public Key B to A
  5. E intercepts Public Key B
  6. E sends Public Key E to A
  7. A encrypts MA with PubE and sends half of mA to B
  8. E intercepts and send without modification to B
  9. B encrypts MB with PubE and sends half of mB to A
  10. E intercepts and send without modification to A
  11. A sends second half of encrypted (with E) mA to B
  12. E intercepts and sends without modification to B
  13. B sends second half of encrypted (with E) mB to A
  14. E intercepts and sends without modification to B
  15. E can decrypt the messages, as they are encrypted with E
  16. A attempts to decrypt with PrivA, but output is garbage
  17. B attempts to decrypt with PrivB, but output is garbage

The power of this protocol rests in its disparate partioning of any message into two interlocked messages that cannot be decrypted separately. Therefore, the intercepting agent Eve cannot eavesdrop discretely because she cannot allow messages to pass through her seamlessly as before. If she wishes to understand the contents of a message, she must decrypt both parts together and thus can no longer pass along the messages unperturbed. When one Alice or Bob go to decrypt a diad of messages, the interlocking will now be scrambled and the message will be rendered meaningless, thus betraying the presence of an eavesdropping agent. Once Bob and Alice know their line of communication is no longer safe, they may then take action to fine a new secure line for proper private communication.
